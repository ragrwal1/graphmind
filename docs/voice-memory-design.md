## Problem Statement

You run two living datasets: rich company profiles and a separate people/investor memory. You want a voice-first capture path where spoken notes become durable, attributable memory (not just transcripts), with correct handling of hard proper nouns, entity identity across spelling variants, temporal truth (raised vs raising), and downstream workflows: (1) surface companies when profiles change in ways that match thesis or priorities, (2) suggest investors when companies are added or materially updated.

The core risk is not the microphone UI. It is the memory layer: evidence vs memory, versioning, conflict handling, entity resolution, and retrieval that blends structured attributes with fuzzy relationship context.

## What Makes This Cool

If it works, you get an always-on associate that never silently corrupts the CRM: every profile change traces to evidence, weird names stop poisoning the graph, and recommendations read like diligence memos ("because claim_… and edge_…") instead of vibes.

## Constraints

- Human scale: ~60 investors, ~600 companies, ~1000 cap. Brute-force scoring across the full set is cheap.
- Airtable remains the team-editable CRM; engineers should not fight Airtable for versioned AI memory.
- Names are a first-class product problem: STT errors must not create duplicate entities.
- Changing Airtable bases/fields is normal; field mapping must be config-driven.

## Premises

1. **Evidence is immutable.** Raw audio, raw transcript, corrected transcript, extraction outputs, and links to prompts used for STT all live in a `Source` / `voice_notes` trail. Profiles are derived, not overwritten in place without provenance.
2. **Memory is claim-first, not summary-first.** The LLM never directly mutates "the company row" as authority. It proposes `Claim` rows; promotion to profile state is explicit (auto when safe + high confidence, else queue).
3. **Entity resolution is a two-step pipeline.** Context-packed STT vocabulary (ranked, small) plus post-hoc repair/disambiguation against canonical entities and aliases. Low confidence routes to a lightweight human confirm, not silent inserts.
4. **Temporal semantics live in claims.** "Raising" and "raised" are different predicates or different `valid_from`/`valid_to` windows. Pivots are modeled as superseding claims, not silent overwrites.
5. **At this scale, skip a graph database.** Postgres tables for entities and `entity_edges` plus pgvector + SQL filters are enough until you feel real pain.
6. **Airtable is source for "what the team says today"; Supabase is source for "what changed, why, and what the machine inferred."** Sync is periodic and hash-based, not faux-real-time enterprise streaming.

Agree/disagree: treat the above as the working contract for v1. Revise in a follow-up session if any premise fails for your compliance or tooling reality.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Minimal MVP (fastest spike)

**Summary:** Airtable + Vercel + OpenAI only. Voice upload, STT with a name prompt built from Airtable fetches, LLM extraction into Airtable "AI Review" rows. No dedicated mirror DB.

**Effort:** S  
**Risk:** Medium (duplicates, weak history, embedding/recommendation history gets painful quickly)

**Pros:**

- Ships in days; no Supabase schema work.
- Ops surface stays entirely in Airtable for non-engineers.

**Cons:**

- Versioning, soft deletes, and recommendation provenance fight Airtable field limits and record history UX.
- Harder to guarantee "never overwrite evidence" invariants.

**Reuses:** Existing Airtable bases and automations if any.

### Approach B: "Tiny reliable internal tool" (recommended)

**Summary:** Next.js on Vercel, Supabase Postgres (+ pgvector + Storage), Airtable as edited CRM via mapped sync, OpenAI for STT (prompted), repair, extraction, embeddings, and rerank. `jobs` table or cron for sync and recompute. `entity_edges` in Postgres instead of Neo4j.

**Effort:** M  
**Risk:** Low to medium (mostly engineering discipline, not infra complexity)

**Pros:**

- Clean separation: Airtable for humans, Supabase for machine memory and audits.
- Event-ish model: `voice_notes`, `claims`, `snapshots`, `recommendations` with hashes and soft deletes.
- At N=60 and M=600, full-grid scoring and explain trails are tractable.

**Cons:**

- You own sync correctness (hash drift, mapping mistakes).
- Two systems to keep aligned mentally.

**Reuses:** Your proposed table list and sync algorithm; standard Vercel cron / Supabase schedules.

### Approach C: Push graph + job orchestration early

**Summary:** Everything in B, plus Trigger.dev (or similar) from day one, and possibly a dedicated graph store or Graphiti-style service for temporal edges.

**Effort:** L  
**Risk:** Medium (more moving parts before you have end-to-end voice loops validated)

**Pros:**

- Cleaner long-running pipelines and retries when extraction chains grow.
- Future-proofs if relationship queries become first-class product features.

**Cons:**

- Slower time-to-learning from real voice notes.
- Overkill until extraction and entity resolution are measurably stable.

**Reuses:** Same core tables as B; adds orchestration layer.

## Recommended Approach

**Choose Approach B** because it matches your scale, your stated split of Airtable vs Supabase responsibilities, and your phased MVP (v1 manual review, v2 embeddings and snapshots, v3 edges and feedback) without paying graph-DB tax early.

Concrete first build (MVP v1):

1. **Tables (Supabase):** `airtable_sources`, `airtable_records`, `companies`, `investors`, `voice_notes`, `claims`, `recommendations`, `sync_runs` (add `company_snapshots` / `investor_snapshots` as soon as you promote claims automatically).
2. **Voice path:** upload audio to Storage; build ranked name pack (recent touches, active pipeline, workflow-scoped entities, aliases); STT with prompt; repair + resolve; extract structured claims; store all layers; enqueue Airtable review row for low confidence or material fields.
3. **Sync path:** paginated Airtable pull every 15-60 minutes; normalize; `raw_hash` compare; changed rows update mirror, snapshot, re-embed when you turn embeddings on; missing rows soft-deactivate.
4. **Matching path:** start literal: score all investors vs one company and vice versa using hybrid hand rules (tags, hard excludes) plus one structured LLM rerank call; persist `reason_codes` and `supporting_claim_ids` on `recommendations`.
5. **Triggers:** on accepted claims or significant hash change, mark `material_change` and recompute small candidate sets (not the whole universe if you later grow past 1k).

## Open Questions

- **Authority model:** Which predicates can auto-write back to Airtable vs always sit in a review queue? (Fundraising status and segment are high stakes.)
- **Speaker identity:** Single user (you) vs multi-user org; affects `speaker` and permissions on confirm queue.
- **Merge workflow:** When two Airtable records collapse or a company reappears with a new record id, what is the canonical merge UX?
- **Privacy/retention:** How long raw audio is kept; whether some claims should be "internal only" and never sync outward.

## Success Criteria

- **STT:** For a curated test set of 50 hard names from your CRM, word error rate improves measurably vs unprompted baseline; repair pass resolves >90% to correct existing entities without duplicate creation (target; tune with data).
- **Memory:** 100% of promoted profile fields trace to at least one `claim_id` or `airtable_sync` snapshot id.
- **Workflows:** Given a synthetic pivot note, the system produces ranked investor suggestions where every top-5 line item cites structured reasons you can audit in under 2 minutes.
- **Ops:** Airtable deletion does not delete machine memory; reactivation works; false merge rate stays near zero because ambiguous cases hit the confirm queue.
